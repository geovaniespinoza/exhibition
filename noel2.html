Child Labor Poem

The past and the present How different are the past and the present How similar are the past and the present In the past The first factory in the united states was opened in 1790 The first school opened in America was in the 17th century In the present Millions of factories are still up and operational in the united states Over 246,000 thousand schools are operating in the united states In the past Schools taught children to read and write In the present Schools are outdated systems that train our youth to become factory workers The sky’s the limit they tell you Then for the next fifteen years they tell you how to act How to think Where to sit When to speak When to eat What to wear Eight hours a day 9 to 5 Single file line Raise your hand to speak Involuntary Manslaughter Killing all creativity and individuality since 1635 An ongoing case the people of the jury refuse to look into Freedom of expression is stripped away from us because her shoulders are to distracting and inappropriate and this is what they think we should wear Freedom of speech is taken away from us when I can’t have a conversation with my teacher because legally they can't talk about their stance about politics or religion because that would mean giving teachers some form of individuality Your creativity is nothing more that just a number on point system, your creativity is nothing more than a letter grade, your creativity is nothing more than just something that someone grades based on how well you think like them ‘ A system for the people by the people where everyone's supposed to be equal But for some reason we haven't come up with a squeal
